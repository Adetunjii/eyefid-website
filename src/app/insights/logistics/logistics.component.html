DON’T START THAT LOGISTICS BUSINESS UNTIL YOU READ THIS

This is an interview with the head of operations of one of the many successful dispatch logistics brands in Lagos, Nigeria. He has voluntarily shared his experience about his start up journey. Where he shares with us everything you need to know about the logistics business and how to succeed in the business. Tell us about the dispatch logistics industry The industry is a large one but personally, my view of the industry is centered around our operation. We are still at the surface and there is more to logistics than meet the eyes. I will rather you call it Dispatch business for the sake of the scale and level we are in. when you look critically at the market share and market size of the logistics industry, bike dispatch is the smallest aspect of what the sector entails. The industry is a very large one of which many people go into with little idea about the industry instead of creating a plan. It requires so much effort and dedication to succeed in Logistics. It is a highly risky business with high report of bike accidents, there are insurance but not enough to cater for the dangers of the business. No matter how much you try to be careful, the roads are bad and everyone is on the run trying to get to their various destination in the quickest possible way. You must think twice before going into it.

What Made you go into this business?

At the fast pace of growth in the real estate industry there I saw an opportunity beyond the normal conventional real estate agent. I saw that relocation was a serious issue in the real estate industry where people buy houses and need to move out from one home to a new home. I was passionate about proffering the solution but also realized that it was a capital-intensive project so I decided to start small. I began the dispatch business which is the lowest point of the logistics industry.

Did you Undergo any form of training before starting?

No, just have a business plan! Right before I set up my business and registered my company, I had previously worked for a friend’s dispatch business for 2 months to know how orders are received, how negotiations are done with the riders and how to manage riders. I knew I needed to have a solid understanding with the marketing aspect of the business and I was learning from online sources as well. If there is one thing that has brough us this far, it is asking question. I kept asking questions from those who were there before me and I learnt from their mistakes. I joined a logistics company group chat in Lagos were people shared their experiences ranging from how riders treat their employers and they also shared valuable tips on insurance, where bikes are repaired, the necessary papers, how to go about branding, keeping up with your customers etc.

What lesson did you learn in your first six months of starting the business?

That was the toughest time of my whole life. You know when you are starting a new business in a new industry feeling all excited calculating all the profits you are about to rake off and the imagination of your daily income and all of a sudden you realize that it was all a fantasy. My first six month I had the struggle of trying to win customers, trying to prove that I am trust worthy and capable of delivering on my promise. It was never easy, there were loses but I was resilient. I had the issue of handling riders who are hardwired to be irresponsible. In the first three month it felt like I was doing nothing as there were no orders. At that time people were skeptical about the brand. Some times when I am lucky, I get four orders in a day which I had to beg for and I still had to pay the riders from my personal money regardless. But as time went on from the fifth month things got better as I started waking up to 10 pending orders. It was an opportunity to prove ourselves.

If there was something you could have done correctly in the six month what would it have been?

As a starter you are desperate to be out there, desperate to make money, desperate to get moving and desperate not to make mistakes. At the beginning I was too careful not to make mistakes but it led me nowhere. It was until I freed myself and dared to fail and also face my fears that I got better. This is something I wish I knew in the first six month. I also wish I wasn’t desperate. Desperation will make you meet a rider today and want him to start work instantly without proper interview and background checks. But now I have my contract, guarantors form, Job description, shared organisation’s objectives and many legalities that puts the riders on check. I also ensure that they are mentally sound and okay. Your riders will eventually be a representation of your brand!

What would you say is the key attribute to your business success?

First of all, your customer service. I will always tell people, the name of the business is nothing, the business is the rider, the admin or customer relationship officer and how they communicate with your customer. They determine the success of the business; they are the people that your customers see and interact with. When these people are messed up your business is on the bad side. One thing that has helped box2door is that customer relationship became top notch and something we could brag about. We good excellent feedbacks from most clients. Riders being able to deliver at the right time, riders that see the success of the company as their successes. Sometimes client give feedbacks such as” your rider was really funny today and I want him to make my other deliveries “that was a sign he was passionate about delivering value which uplifts the brand image.

If you would give any advice to someone willing to go into this line of business, what would it be?

My advice would be for you to make research and ask questions. Don’t go about believing stories about the business because people are going to tell you it is a very lucrative business but they don’t tell you about the hard work and effort that is required to go into this business. I have had people come to me to ask how I am doing it and how they are tired and can’t continue barely after a month. My advice would be that you should be passionate about the business. I understand that you are here for the money but that will and should come after you have offered value. The primary goal should be to make your customers happy and satisfied with your service and money will follow.

Capital required to start

If you don’t have so much money you will suffer and might go about the wrong way for managing the business. It requires having extra money for insurgencies. The industry is risky. Starting off I had issues where my first two bikes had accident at the same time. It was this extra money kept aside that kept the business moving.
